## Core Idea

Habits are the compound interest of self-improvement. Getting 1% better every day counts for a lot in the long run.

## The Four Laws of Behavior Change

### 1. Make It Obvious

- Design your environment so the cues for good habits are obvious and visible.
- Implementation intention: "I will [behavior] at [time] in [location]."
- Habit stacking: "After [current habit], I will [new habit]."

### 2. Make It Attractive

- Pair an action you want to do with an action you need to do (temptation bundling).
- Join a culture where your desired behavior is the normal behavior.

### 3. Make It Easy

- Reduce friction. Decrease the number of steps between you and your good habits.
- Two-minute rule: when you start a new habit, it should take less than two minutes to do.
- Automate your habits whenever possible.

### 4. Make It Satisfying

- Give yourself an immediate reward when you complete your habit.
- Use a habit tracker. Don't break the chain.
- Never miss twice. Missing once is a mistake. Missing twice is the start of a new habit.

## Key Quotes
